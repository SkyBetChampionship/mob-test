THE CHAMPIONSHIP'S HISTORY

Previously, the Football League Championship has been called Division 2 (1892-1992) and The First Division (1992-2004). The name was changed to Football League Championship ahead of the 2004-05 season.

The Championship is the wealthiest non-top flight football division in the world, and is the seventh richest division in European football.

Barnsley have spent more seasons at the second level of English football than any other team. On 3 January 2011, The Tykes became the first team to win 1,000 wins in the second tier of English football.

Sunderland won the first ever 'Championship' in 2004-05, with Wigan Athletic finishing second. West Ham United won the first ever Championship Play-Off Final that season, following a 1-0 victory over Preston North End at the Millennium Stadium.

Reading hold the record for the most points achieved in a single Championship season. In the 2005-06 season, The Royals racked up 106 points.

On 4 May 2007, Leeds United became the first side since the rebranding to the Championship to enter administration. They were deducted 10 points, which eventually saw them relegated.

On 28 May 2007, Derby County became the first side to win a Championship Play Off Final at the new Wembley Stadium, with a 1-0 victory over West Bromwich Albion.

In September 2009, Coca Cola announced they would end their sponsorship of the Championship, and the Football League. In March 2010, npower were announced as the new sponsors of the Football League for 3 seasons. In July 2013, Sky Bet announced that they would sponsor the league.

24 May 2014 saw the highest attendance for any Championship fixture. 87,348 people witnessed a stoppage time winner by Bobby Zamora to seal QPR's promotion to the Premier League, and heartbreak for Derby County.

Ahead of the 2016-17 season, the Football League was rebranded as the English Football League (EFL). That season, Rotherham United recorded the lowest points tally in Championship history - achieving just 23 points from their 46 matches.

In the 2012-13 season, Peterborough United were relegated with 54 points. This is the highest tally achieved by a relegated side in Championship history.
